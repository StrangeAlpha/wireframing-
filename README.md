# Low-Fidelity Wireframes
Low-fidelity wireframes are simple, quick, and conceptual.
They focus on layout and information hierarchy rather than design details.

# High-Fidelity Wireframes
High-fidelity wireframes are detailed and polished, closely resembling the final product.

# Figma
Figma is one of the most popular and versatile tools for wireframing and UI/UX design. It’s a browser-based, collaborative design platform that allows teams to create, share, and iterate on wireframes and prototypes in real time.

# Key Features
1. Real-Time Collaboration: Multiple team members can work on the same file simultaneously, making it ideal for remote teams.
2. Cross-Platform Accessibility: Works directly in your web browser or as a desktop app across macOS, Windows, and Linux.
3. Reusable Components: Create and maintain design systems with consistent buttons, icons, and layouts.
4. Prototyping Capabilities: Turn static wireframes into interactive prototypes without switching tools.
5. Commenting and Feedback: Stakeholders can leave comments directly on designs for faster iteration.

# Why Wireframe
Wireframes play a crucial role in guiding the design process and improving communication among team members by serving as a visual blueprint for a product’s structure and user experience. They help teams align early, make informed design decisions, and reduce costly revisions later in development.

1. Guiding the Design Process
Wireframes act as a visual roadmap for the project, illustrating layout, hierarchy, and functionality before visual design or coding begins.
- Low-fidelity wireframes guide the early stages of design by focusing on layout and user flow rather than aesthetics. For example, a designer might sketch simple boxes to represent navigation menus, buttons, and content areas   to test where elements should go quickly. This helps teams explore multiple layout options quickly and make structural decisions early.
- High-fidelity wireframes, on the other hand, guide later design stages by refining visuals and interactions. They show colors, typography, and interactive components, helping developers understand exactly how the product       should look and behave. For instance, in Figma, a high-fidelity prototype can simulate user navigation, allowing designers to test usability before handing off to developers.
  By moving from low to high fidelity, teams can progressively refine both the form (visuals) and function (interaction) of the design, ensuring each decision builds on the last.

2. Facilitating Communication Among Team Members
Wireframes are also powerful communication tools that bridge the gap between designers, developers, and stakeholders.
- They provide a shared visual reference, ensuring everyone interprets the project’s direction consistently. Instead of discussing abstract ideas, teams can refer to a concrete layout. For example, a product manager can review   a low-fidelity wireframe in Figma to verify that all essential features are included before visual design begins.
- Collaborative tools like Figma enhance this communication by allowing real-time co-editing and commenting. Developers can leave comments directly on specific elements, designers can make instant updates, and stakeholders can   provide feedback without long email threads or misinterpretations.
- Wireframes also make usability testing and feedback loops more efficient.Early low-fidelity sketches can be tested quickly with users to validate workflows, while high-fidelity versions can later confirm visual appeal and      clarity.
